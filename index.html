01 — TECHNOLOGY

What is
PainSync?

PainSync is a wearable thermochemical biosensor — an ultra-thin patch engineered with encapsulated liquid crystal compounds that undergo a precise, reproducible color shift in response to localized temperature changes caused by inflammatory pain.

Unlike subjective pain scales or expensive imaging equipment, PainSync delivers objective, real-time chromatic feedback directly on the patient's skin — visible to the naked eye and quantifiable via companion optical analysis.

02 — MECHANISM

How It Works

Three precise stages convert invisible thermal signals into actionable clinical data.

01

Thermal Detection

Encapsulated liquid crystal molecules within the patch matrix respond to surface-level temperature gradients as low as 0.1°C — capturing even subtle inflammatory signatures before they become clinically apparent.

Liquid Crystal Thermography
02

Chromatic Synchronization

As thermal energy disrupts the helical pitch of the cholesteric liquid crystal lattice, reflected wavelengths shift across the visible spectrum — from deep violet at baseline to cyan and white at peak inflammatory response.

Temperature → Color Translation
03

Visual Mapping

The resulting chromatic pattern forms a spatial heatmap of pain distribution on the skin surface. Clinicians can assess severity, location, and spread with immediate precision — or capture it optically for digital analysis.

Medical Precision Mapping
