What is it

PainSync patches use liquid crystal technology to detect localized heat gradients, converting biological pain signals into a precise visual color map — all without a single needle.

Thermochemical Detection

Liquid crystal microstructures respond to temperature deltas as small as 0.3°C, detecting sub-dermal inflammation patterns with clinical precision.

How it Works

Three steps.
One clear answer.

From skin contact to clinical map — the PainSync system compresses a complex diagnostic workflow into a single chromatic moment.

  1. 01
    Thermal detection by liquid crystals

    Cholesteric liquid crystals within the patch matrix detect localized skin temperature variations caused by underlying inflammation, neural activation, or circulatory changes. The crystals' helical pitch shifts in direct response to thermal gradients.

  2. 02
    Chromatic synchronization

    Temperature-to-color translation occurs through Bragg diffraction — each temperature range produces a unique, reproducible wavelength. Violet and blue indicate lower temperatures, while orange and red signal higher thermal activity correlated with pain intensity.

  3. 03
    Visual mapping for medical precision

    The resulting chromatic pattern is a spatially-resolved pain map. Clinicians photograph or scan the patch output — a standardized chromatic scale then quantifies pain location, gradient spread, and severity with 73% greater diagnostic accuracy than subjective self-reporting.
